Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is loved by people all over the world. It is a baked pasta dish that is made with layers of wide, flat pasta noodles, tomato sauce, meat, and cheese. The layers are typically repeated several times, creating a hearty and satisfying meal.

The noodles are boiled until they are tender and then layered in a baking dish with tomato sauce, meat, and cheese. The tomato sauce is often seasoned with garlic, onions, and herbs, such as basil and oregano, which give it a rich and savory flavor. The meat used can be ground beef, sausage, or a combination of both, and it is typically cooked with the tomato sauce to create a flavorful and hearty filling.

In addition to the meat and tomato sauce, cheese is an important ingredient in lasagna. Ricotta cheese is often used, which is a soft and creamy cheese that adds a delicious flavor and texture to the dish. Other cheeses, such as mozzarella, parmesan, and provolone, can also be used to add a rich and cheesy flavor to the lasagna.

Once all of the layers have been assembled, the lasagna is baked in the oven until it is hot and bubbly. The top layer of cheese becomes golden and crispy, creating a delicious crust that adds to the overall texture of the dish.

Lasagna is a comfort food that is perfect for serving to family and friends. It is a filling and delicious meal that is sure to satisfy even the heartiest of appetites. Whether served with a side salad or garlic bread, lasagna is a classic dish that will always be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef, sausage, or a combination of both
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Dried basil
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Provolone cheese
  • Egg
  • Olive oil
